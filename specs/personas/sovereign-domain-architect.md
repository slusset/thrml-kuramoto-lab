# Persona: Sovereign Domain Architect

## Role

An architect or domain team member responsible for a clinically meaningful
bounded context and its dependable operation within a larger health system.

## Goals

- Give the domain clear authority over its model, reasoning, data lifecycle,
  deployable configuration, and operations.
- Preserve alignment with shared clinical outcomes, safety constraints, and
  interoperability commitments.
- Evaluate non-centralized architecture proposals with reproducible evidence
  rather than metaphor or organizational preference alone.
- Make boundaries visible through contracts, tests, telemetry, and ownership.

## Frustrations

- “Autonomy” is interpreted as isolation or rejection of standards.
- “Governance” is implemented as centralized control over local design.
- Internal health metrics are treated as proof of clinical or organizational
  outcome alignment.
- Architecture decisions are debated abstractly without small, falsifiable
  pilots or explicit tradeoffs.

## Context

- Tech comfort: high
- Usage frequency: occasional research, recurring architecture decisions
- Key devices: desktop
- Operating environment: regulated healthcare with shared platforms, clinical
  governance, and multiple interacting bounded contexts

## Quotes

> “A sovereign domain is not isolated. It owns its internal coherence and its
> boundaries, and it remains accountable to the field it serves.”
