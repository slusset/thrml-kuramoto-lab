# Story: Trace an Experiment to an Architecture Decision

## Narrative

As a sovereign domain architect,
I want each architectural interpretation to trace to a specific experiment and
its evidence boundary,
So that I can use the lab to design a reversible pilot without presenting a
model analogy as clinical or organizational proof.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] The decision states the bounded context, local ownership, and external
  telos or constraint.
- [ ] It references a named experiment family from the catalog.
- [ ] It distinguishes the measured result from the architectural inference.
- [ ] It states the evidence level and at least one way the analogy can fail.
- [ ] It names real operational or clinical evidence that would support or
  reject the decision.
- [ ] It includes both internal-coherence and external-alignment measures.
- [ ] It identifies a bounded pilot and a rollback or revision path.

## Notes

The purpose is disciplined translation, not a claim that organizations are
oscillators or that one topology is universally correct.
